\section{Introduction}
The accuracy of building energy models is highly dependent on the fidelity of occupancy schedules. This dossier provides the empirical justification for the multi-sensor fusion methodology used in the SDH Level 4 simulation, establishing a defensible audit trail from raw telemetry to EnergyPlus compact schedules.

\section{Methodological Framework}
The pipeline uses a deterministic weighting of $CO_2$, Light, and PIR signals to resolve the "stillness blind" of motion sensors and the "diffusion lag" of air quality sensors.

\section{Empirical Calibration Results}
\subsection{Metabolic Lag Derivation}
Atmospheric $CO_2$ accumulation is a byproduct of mass balance. Based on 454 arrival events across the KETI dataset, the median lead of visual triggers (Light) over $CO_2$ rise was established at **22 minutes**.
\begin{equation}
S_{CO_2}^{\text{sync}}(t) = S_{CO_2}(t - 22)
\end{equation}

\subsection{Sensor Independence Metrics}
To validate that $CO_2$ and Light provide complementary lift, we calculate the Adjusted Rand Index (ARI).
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{ARI:} 0.007 (Confirming high sensor independence)
    \item \textbf{NMI:} 0.144 (Low shared structural information)
\end{itemize}
